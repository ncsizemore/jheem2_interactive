Melissa Schnure is a PhD student in the Department of Epidemiology at the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health. Her current work primarily focuses on modeling the integration of HIV and non-communicable diseases in the sub-Saharan African context. Melissa has previously worked on HIV prevention modeling, specifically for voluntary medical male circumcision and oral pre-exposure prophylaxis. She holds a Master of Science in Infectious Disease Epidemiology from Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health and a Bachelor of Arts in Ecology and Evolutionary Biology from Princeton University.
